My Weekly Learnings

Week I

I learned how to give a proper self-introduction. I understood how to structure my introduction by including my name, education, achievements, and hobbies. I also learned:

  • The importance of speaking with confidence.
  • How to use good body language and eye contact.
  • Basics of verbal and non-verbal communication.
  • How to end the introduction politely and professionally.
  • These learnings helped improve my communication and presentation skills.

Week II

This week, I focused on identifying my strengths and areas for improvement in communication. I became more confident but also realized I need to work on eye contact and fluency. The activities gave me chances to grow and face challenges that helped improve my skills.

Strengths

  • Gained confidence in speaking.
  • Improved idea structuring.
  • Better stage presence.

Weaknesses

  • Nervousness during long speech.
  • Difficulty with eye contact.
  • Limited vocabulary.

Opportunities

  • Practice through group activities.
  • Feedback from teachers.
  • Learning by observing others.

Challenges

  • Overcoming stage fear.
  • Speaking without hesitation.
  • Managing time while speaking.

Week III

In Week III, we created a role play on a surprise birthday. During this activity, I learned:

  • How to coordinate and communicate effectively in a group.
  • The importance of teamwork and creativity in role play.
  • How to express emotions naturally while performing.
  • To handle unexpected situations and respond confidently.
  • That role play improves imagination, speaking fluency, and confidence in front of an audience.

Week IV

In Week IV, I performed the role play activity on a surprise birthday celebration. This was a fun and interactive session where we acted out different roles in the scenario. Through this role play, I learned:

  • How to express myself more confidently in front of others.
  • The importance of clear voice, expressions, and gestures while acting.
  • How to work as a team and support each other during the performance.
  • The value of creativity and imagination in making the role play interesting.
  • That practicing role play helps improve fluency, quick thinking, and stage confidence.
