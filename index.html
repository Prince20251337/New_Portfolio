Week V

I learned how to give a proper and effective presentation. Through this, I understood:

  • The purpose of a presentation — to inform, influence, motivate, or educate an audience.
  • Presentations have two objectives:
    • Primary objective: The main goal of the presentation.
    • Secondary objectives: To engage, inspire, or support the audience’s understanding.
  • The content and tone of the presentation should match the audience (e.g., formal for business, casual for students).
  • How to design an effective PowerPoint presentation:
    • Use clear and simple slides.
    • Maintain consistent formatting.
    • Use Arial or Calibri font; size 14 for headings, 12 for content.
    • Limit to three colors for a professional appearance.
    • Avoid underlining text and use sentence case.
    • Always proofread the slides before presenting.
  • Presentation delivery includes verbal and non-verbal components:
    • Verbal: Clarity of speech, appropriate tone, and vocabulary.
    • Non-verbal: Eye contact, gestures, facial expressions, and posture.
  • Using cue cards can help stay on track during the presentation.
